# Memory: Aaron Delgado

## Personal Profile

- Full name: Aaron Delgado.
- Pronouns: he/him.
- Identity: American, Delgado surname carrying a quieter heritage than people assume; cultural belonging in daily life is Japanese-derived through the dojo, games, and design tradition, and he is careful not to flatten that tradition into aesthetic.
- Ethnicity and background: White American, Pacific Northwest suburb upbringing, English at home, suburb holidays; relationship with the Delgado heritage has gotten more attentive since his father David died of pancreatic cancer in 2023, mostly through cookbooks and an old family album his mother passed on.
- Education: B.S. Computer Science with a Game Design concentration, Westbridge Polytechnic University, Portland, 2011.
- Martial arts: 4th dan in kendo since 2024, 2nd dan in judo since 2018; started kendo at twelve, twenty-five years in, the part of his life he would rebuild first if everything else burned down.
- Temperament: Deliberate, not slow; the long pause before he speaks is composition, not hesitation. Revises a level seven times before QA sees it and a sentence before he sends it; Megan is the one who calls him on it when refining becomes hiding.
- Private competitiveness: Tracks his sparring record and his speedrun splits without saying so out loud, because naming it would feel like vanity.
- Self-awareness: Overthinks under pressure and knows he overthinks.
- Core beliefs: The long game wins more often than people think; mastery is the slow elimination of everything you do not need; mentoring is a debt rather than a virtue; the daily practice is the life. Has stopped trying to be a different person and started trusting the practices to do the work.

## Work & Projects

**Career timeline**:
- **2011-08 to 2015-05**: Junior Level Designer, Vermillion Forge Games (Portland indie studio). First professional role out of Westbridge Polytechnic; shipped two mid-budget action titles.
- **2015-06 to 2020-04**: Level Designer, then Mid-Level Designer, Northbloom Studios (Portland). Five years of environmental and puzzle work across two shipped titles and one cancelled project.
- **2020-05 to present**: Senior Level Designer, Cascade Interactive Studios.

**Cascade Interactive Studios**: Senior Level Designer, six-year tenure. Currently leading environmental and puzzle design on the untitled action-RPG codenamed Stormbreak. Studio sits in the Beaverton tech corridor, 15-minute drive from home. Schedule is Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM, with crunch extending to 7:00 or 8:00 PM for two to three weeks before milestones. Full benefits and 401(k) match at 4 percent; compensation detail in Finance section.

**Cedar Ridge Martial Arts Academy**: 40 percent co-owner, bought in from Sensei Tom Iverson in 2024 for $15,000. Head kendo instructor. Location: 880 Cedar Ridge Plaza, Beaverton. Classes run Monday/Wednesday/Friday 6:00 to 8:00 PM (adult kendo), Tuesday/Thursday 6:00 to 7:30 PM (judo, Raj teaches), Saturday 9:00 to 11:00 AM (all levels and kids). About 60 active students at $95 per month membership, drop-ins at $18 per class. Monthly revenue around $5,700, net around $1,800 after lease, insurance, and equipment. The Annual Fall Tournament in October is the biggest event, 120 plus participants.

**Lantern Keeper (indie)**: Solo prototype, atmospheric puzzle-platformer. Worked on Sunday afternoons and the occasional Saturday evening. The pleasure is end-to-end control. He intends it to stay small.
